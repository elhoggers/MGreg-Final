Mark Gregory

Mark founded M Gregory Legal in 2024, to build on his 30 years’ experience in public law, native title, planning and resources law.

Mark specialises in commercial aspects of public law and land access law, such as drafting native title contracts, advising local governments, advising on planning law and assisting with land compensation matters.

He also litigates in various specialist areas, including land compensation, planning (State Administrative Tribunal reviews), local government prosecutions and proceedings before the Mining Warden.

During the 1990s, Mark worked at the Aboriginal Legal Service of WA preparing Western Australia’s first litigated native title claim. In 1998 he joined a national firm, later becoming a partner at that firm and heading the Perth native title and local government practices.

Mark’s practice today focuses on the land access needs of his clients, including obtaining land tenure, mining tenements and approvals. He helps clients protect their property assets, and optimise the value of those assets, in areas such as planning, native title and other regulatory regimes.
